Fish Schooling

Emergent collective behavior from 3 local rules. Each fish only senses its neighbors — no global coordination exists.

The Three Rules
Separation 1.5
Avoid crowding neighbors — push away from fish that get too close.
Alignment 1.0
Steer toward the average heading of nearby fish.
Cohesion 1.0
Steer toward the average position of nearby fish.
Perception
Visual Range 80
How far each fish can see. Larger range = more global awareness.
Separation Radius 25
Minimum personal space. Fish closer than this are pushed away.
Field of View 270°
Angle of vision. 360° = all-around sight. Lower = blind spots behind.
Movement
Max Speed 3.5
Maximum velocity of each fish (pixels/frame).
Max Steering Force 0.08
How sharply fish can turn. Low = sluggish turns, high = erratic.
Population 150
Number of fish. Changes take effect on reset.
